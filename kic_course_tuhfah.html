Nun Sakin & Tanween

Nun Sakin Rules

Izhar & Idgham

🔊 Izhar (Clarity)

Pronounce the Nun clearly without nasal sound.

Throat Letters:

ء هـ ع ح غ خ

🔗 Idgham (Merging)

Merge the Nun into the next letter.

Yarmaloon:

ي ر م ل و ن

Note: No merging if in one single word (e.g., Dunya).

Nun Sakin Rules

Iqlab & Ikhfa

🔄 Iqlab (Conversion)

Convert the Nun into a Hidden Mim with Ghunnah.

Only 1 Letter:

ب

🌫️ Ikhfa (Hiding)

Hide the Nun sound between clear and merged, with Ghunnah.

The Remaining 15:

(Ta, Tha, Jim, Dal, Dhal, Zay, Sin, Shin, Sad, Dad, Ta, Za, Fa, Qaf, Kaf)

Mim & Shaddah

Rules of Mim Sakin

Rule of Shaddah ( ّ ): Any Nun or Mim with a Shaddah requires a strong Ghunnah (2 counts).

Ikhfa Shafawi

Labial Hiding. Before letter Baa (ب).

Idgham

Merging. Before another Mim (م).

Izhar Shafawi

Clarity. Before all other letters.

⚠️ Be careful not to hide Mim before Waw or Fa!

The Definite Article

Rules of Lam (Al-)

🌑 Lam Qamariya (Lunar)

Pronounced Clearly.

Before 14 letters (e.g., Al-Qamar, Al-Kitab).

"Seek your goal and futile pursuits repel"

☀️ Lam Shamsiyya (Solar)

Merged (Silent Lam).

Before remaining 14 letters (e.g., Al-Shams, Al-Rahman).

*Verbal Lams (in verbs) are always pronounced clearly.

Advanced

Letter Relationships

Mithlayn

Identical letters (e.g. Baa meets Baa).

Mutaqaribayn

Close in articulation & attributes.

Mutajanisayn

Same articulation, different attributes.

General Rule: If the first is silent (Sakin), merge it into the second.

Introduction to Madd

ا و ي

Madd Asli (Natural)

Baseline length (2 counts).

Not followed by Hamza or Sukoon.

Madd Far'i (Derived)

Extra length caused by a reason.

Reasons: Hamza (ء) or Sukoon (ْ).

Caused by Hamza

Derived Madd Types

Madd Muttasil

Connected (Same word).

Mandatory (4-5 counts).

Madd Munfasil

Separated (Next word).

Allowed (2, 4-5 counts).

Madd Badal

Hamza precedes Madd letter.

2 counts.

Caused by Sukoon

Madd Lazim (Compulsory)

6

Must be elongated 6 counts. Caused by a permanent Sukoon.

Kalimi (Word)

Occurs in words (e.g. Daallaleen).

Harfi (Letter)

Occurs in opening letters (e.g. Alif Lam Meem).
